Pathology Center

Diabetes Mellitus

High Prevalence

A chronic condition where the body cannot effectively regulate blood glucose.

Type 1: Insulin deficiency (autoimmune)
Type 2: Insulin resistance (lifestyle/genetic)

Thyroid Disorders

Metabolic Focus

Dysfunction in the production of T3 and T4 hormones.

Hypothyroidism: Fatigue, weight gain, cold sensitivity.
Hyperthyroidism: Anxiety, weight loss, heat intolerance.

Addison’s Disease

Adrenal Insufficiency

Primary adrenal insufficiency where the body produces insufficient cortisol.

warning Symptoms include hyperpigmentation and extreme fatigue.

Cushing Syndrome

Hypercortisolism

Results from exposure to high levels of cortisol for a long time.

warning Common signs: weight gain in the trunk and face, skin thinning.

Diagnostic Roadmap

1

Laboratory Assays

Blood serum tests including A1C for glucose monitoring, TSH for thyroid function, and specific antibody panels.

2

Advanced Imaging

Ultrasound, MRI, and CT scans to visualize glandular size, nodules, or potential structural abnormalities.

3

Functional Tests

Stimulation and suppression tests (e.g., ACTH stimulation) to evaluate glandular response under pressure.

Treatment Modalities

Insulin Therapy

Crucial for Type 1 management and advanced Type 2 cases.

Hormone Replacement (HRT)

Synthetic hormones like Levothyroxine to restore natural balance.
