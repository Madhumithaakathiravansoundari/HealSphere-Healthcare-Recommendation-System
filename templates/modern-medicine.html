What is Modern Medicine?

Modern medicine, often referred to as conventional or allopathic medicine, is a system in which medical doctors and other healthcare professionals treat symptoms and diseases using drugs, radiation, or surgery.

It relies heavily on the scientific method, prioritizing peer-reviewed evidence and rigorous clinical trials. The philosophy is rooted in biological reductionism—understanding the smallest components of the body to repair the whole system.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is modern medicine superior to traditional methods? expand_more
Modern medicine is designed for scientific reproducibility and acute intervention. While superior for surgery and trauma, many modern practitioners integrate traditional lifestyle methods for chronic preventative care.
How are new treatments approved? expand_more
Treatments undergo rigorous Phase I, II, and III clinical trials to ensure safety and efficacy before being regulated by bodies such as the FDA or EMA.
What is personalized medicine? expand_more
Personalized medicine uses a person's genetic profile to guide decisions made in regard to the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of disease.
