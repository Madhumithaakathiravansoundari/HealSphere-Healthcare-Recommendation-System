The ABCDE Screening Rule

Perform a monthly self-exam. Consult a professional if you notice any of the following in a mole or lesion:

A
Asymmetry: One half of the mole does not match the other.
B
Border: Edges are irregular, ragged, notched, or blurred.
C
Color: The color is not the same all over; may include brown, black, red, white, or blue.
D
Diameter: The spot is larger than 6mm (about the size of a pencil eraser).
E
Evolving: The mole is changing in size, shape, or color.

Clinical FAQ

How often should I see a dermatologist? expand_more
For most adults, an annual full-body skin examination is recommended. However, if you have a personal or family history of skin cancer, or more than 50 moles, your specialist may recommend visits every 3-6 months.
What are the early signs of skin cancer? expand_more
Beyond the ABCDE rules, look for sores that do not heal, pigment that spreads from the border of a spot into surrounding skin, and redness or new swelling beyond the border of a mole.
Can diet significantly impact skin health? expand_more
Yes. High-glycemic diets can exacerbate acne due to insulin spikes, while antioxidant-rich foods and omega-3 fatty acids support cellular repair and inflammatory control.
