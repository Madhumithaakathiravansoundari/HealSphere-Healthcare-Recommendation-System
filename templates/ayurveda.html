Ayurveda: The Science of Life

Ancient Wisdom for Modern Wellness. A holistic healing system originating in India over 5,000 years ago, focusing on the dynamic balance of mind, body, and spirit.

What is Ayurveda?

Ayurveda, often called "the mother of all healing," is a comprehensive system of medicine that emphasizes prevention and personalized health. It posits that every individual is born with a unique genetic constitution known as Prakriti.

The core philosophy is rooted in the belief that health is a state of equilibrium between the three vital forces or doshas. By understanding these energies, practitioners can tailor treatments to the individual's specific needs rather than treating symptoms in isolation.

Limitations & Safety

warning

Acute Trauma & Surgery

Ayurveda is not recommended as a primary treatment for severe acute trauma, infectious epidemics, or conditions requiring immediate surgical intervention.

verified_user

Certification Matters

Always consult a BAMS (Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery) qualified practitioner to ensure herb purity and correct dosage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can Ayurveda be combined with Western medicine? expand_more
Yes, Ayurveda often works complementarily with modern medicine. However, it is vital to disclose all herbal supplements to your MD, as certain herbs like Guggul or Ashwagandha can interact with prescriptions.
How long before I see results? expand_more
Since Ayurveda treats the root cause rather than symptoms, changes are often gradual. While some experience relief in days, chronic imbalances may require 3 to 6 months of dedicated protocol for sustainable results.
