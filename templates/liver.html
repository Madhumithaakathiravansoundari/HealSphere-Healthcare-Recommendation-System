Module 04: Hepatology

The Liver: Your Vital Chemical Factory

The liver is the body's most complex organ after the brain. Acting as a central processing plant, it performs over 500 essential functions including metabolic regulation, blood purification, and critical protein synthesis.

Anatomy Overview

view_quilt

The Lobes

Divided into the larger Right Lobe and smaller Left Lobe, separated by the falciform ligament.

database

Gallbladder

A pear-shaped reservoir that stores and concentrates bile produced by the hepatocytes.

rebase_edit

Hepatic Veins

Carries deoxygenated, filtered blood from the liver to the inferior vena cava.

vitals

Portal Artery

Provides oxygen-rich blood to the liver tissues, ensuring metabolic viability.

Core Biological Roles

Beyond filtration, the liver serves as a metabolic hub for growth and immunity.

cleaning_services
Detoxification

Filters toxins from the blood, including drugs, alcohol, and metabolic waste like ammonia.

architecture
Protein Synthesis

Produces essential proteins for blood clotting (fibrinogen) and fluid balance (albumin).

format_paint
Bile Production

Secrets bile into the small intestine to emulsify fats and facilitate vitamin absorption.

Nutrient Processing Cycle

Flow Dynamics
1. Intake

Nutrients enter via the portal vein from the digestive tract.

arrow_forward
2. Metabolism

Hepatocytes break down glucose, fats, and amino acids.

arrow_forward
3. Storage

Vitamins A, D, E, K, and B12 are stored for future use.

Pathological Overview

Fatty Liver (NAFLD)

Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease

layers

Accumulation of excess fat in liver cells not caused by alcohol consumption, often linked to obesity and diabetes.

Key Symptoms

  • Fatigue
  • Pain in upper right abdomen

Hepatitis (Viral)

Strains A, B, and C

coronavirus

Inflammation of the liver caused by viral infection, leading to potential scarring or chronic failure.

Key Symptoms

  • Jaundice (yellowing)
  • Dark urine

Cirrhosis

Late-stage Scarring

texture

The replacement of liver tissue by scar tissue, eventually preventing the liver from functioning properly.

Key Symptoms

  • Easy bruising
  • Swelling in legs (Edema)

Liver Cancer

Hepatocellular Carcinoma

warning

Malignant tumor that starts in the cells of the liver, often arising from chronic liver disease backgrounds.

Key Symptoms

  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Abdominal swelling

Clinical Diagnostics

lab_profile
Liver Function Tests (LFTs)

Blood tests measuring enzymes (ALT, AST) and proteins (Albumin) to assess overall liver health.

waves
Abdominal Ultrasound

Non-invasive imaging using sound waves to visualize liver structure and detect masses or cysts.

biotech
Liver Biopsy

Extraction of a small tissue sample for microscopic analysis, definitive for staging cirrhosis or cancer.

Management Protocols

Clinical management focuses on halting progression and supporting regenerative capacity.

Pharmacological Interventions expand_more
Includes antiviral medications for Hepatitis, corticosteroids for autoimmune conditions, and diuretics for managing fluid retention (ascites).
Surgical Options expand_more
From resection of localized tumors to full liver transplantation for end-stage liver disease (ESLD).
clinical_notes

Lifestyle Protocols

  • check_circle

    Low-sodium diet to prevent fluid buildup.

  • check_circle

    Complete abstinence from alcohol for alcoholic-related injury.

  • check_circle

    Weight management through aerobic exercise.

Nurturing Your Liver

nutrition
Fiber Intake

High-fiber foods like whole grains and leafy greens help regulate bile flow.

vaccines
Vaccination

Protect against Hepatitis A and B viruses through complete vaccination series.

no_drinks
Moderation

Limit alcohol intake to prevent fatty buildup and permanent oxidative stress.
