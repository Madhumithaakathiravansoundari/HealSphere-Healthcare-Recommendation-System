What is Acupuncture?

Acupuncture is a key component of Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) that has gained global recognition in modern clinical environments. It involves the careful insertion of sterile, hair-thin needles at strategically selected 'Acupoints'.

target

Main Objective

Primarily used for neurological modulation and systemic pain relief.

The Philosophy

Central to its practice is the concept of Qi (vital energy) flowing through Meridians. When this flow is disrupted, illness or pain occurs; acupuncture aims to restore this harmonic balance.

Precautions & Risks

warning

Bleeding Disorders

Increased risk of bruising or excessive bleeding if on anticoagulants.

pregnant_woman

Pregnancy

Certain points can trigger uterine contractions; disclose status immediately.

emergency

Pacemakers

Electro-acupuncture can interfere with electronic cardiac devices.

Not Recommended For:

Active Skin Infections Severe Needle Phobia Acute Emergency Traumas

Frequently Asked Questions

Does it hurt? expand_more
Most patients feel a mild tingling or dull ache, but the needles are so thin that there is generally no sharp pain. Some people find the experience deeply relaxing.
How many sessions will I need? expand_more
While some find immediate relief, chronic issues usually require 6-10 sessions to show significant clinical progress. Your practitioner will design a customized plan.
Is it safe? expand_more
Yes, when performed by a licensed professional using single-use, sterile disposable needles, it is considered one of the safest medical treatments available.
