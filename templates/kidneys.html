Kidneys: Your Filtration Powerhouse

Located just below your rib cage on each side of your spine, the kidneys are sophisticated chemical factories. They process about 200 quarts of blood daily to sift out approximately two quarts of waste products and extra water.

Two thin tubes that carry urine from the kidneys to the bladder.

3

A hollow, muscular organ that stores urine until it's ready to be excreted.

4
Urethra

The canal through which urine passes from the bladder out of the body.

Common Renal Pathologies

Understanding disorders and their clinical presentations.

grain

Kidney Stones (Nephrolithiasis)

Hard deposits of minerals and acid salts that stick together in concentrated urine.

Key Symptoms

  • Severe side/back pain
  • Hematuria (blood in urine)
  • Nausea and vomiting
timeline

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)

A gradual loss of kidney function over time, often caused by diabetes or hypertension.

Key Symptoms

  • Swelling (Edema)
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Changes in urination frequency
warning

Acute Kidney Injury (AKI)

A sudden episode of kidney failure or kidney damage that happens within a few hours or days.

Key Symptoms

  • Sudden oliguria
  • Shortness of breath
  • Confusion or drowsiness

Therapeutic Interventions

From medical management to advanced life-support technologies.

monitor_heart

Hemodialysis

In hemodialysis, blood is pumped out of your body to an artificial kidney machine, and returned to your body by tubes that connect you to the machine.

  • check_circle Usually performed 3 times a week
  • check_circle Lasts about 4 hours per session
  • check_circle Requires surgical access (Fistula/Graft)
home_health

Peritoneal Dialysis

Uses the lining of your abdomen (peritoneum) and a cleaning solution (dialysate) to clean your blood. The solution is washed in and out of your belly.

  • check_circle Can be done at home
  • check_circle Performed daily or nightly while sleeping
  • check_circle More flexible for travel and work

Frequently Asked Questions

How much water should I drink daily for kidney health? expand_more
While the "8 glasses a day" rule is common, the exact amount depends on your age, climate, and activity level. Generally, drinking enough so that your urine is pale yellow or clear is a good indicator of proper hydration.
Can you live with only one kidney? expand_more
Yes, most people can lead a normal, healthy life with one kidney. The single kidney increases in size and capacity to handle the workload of two.
What are the early warning signs of kidney failure? expand_more
Early kidney disease often has no symptoms. However, signs can include persistent itching, muscle cramps, feeling tired, swollen ankles, or foamy urine. Regular screening is essential for those at risk.
