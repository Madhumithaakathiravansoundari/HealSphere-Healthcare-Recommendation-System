Respiratory System

The Lungs: Our Vital Engine

The lungs are the primary organs of respiration, responsible for the vital exchange of gases. They deliver oxygen to your blood and remove carbon dioxide, fueling every cell in your body with the life-sustaining breath of air.

Breathing Process

expand Inspiration

Diaphragm contracts and moves downward, creating a vacuum that pulls air in.

compress Expiration

Diaphragm relaxes and moves upward, forcing carbon dioxide out of the lungs.

Breathing Exercises

self_improvement

Diaphragmatic Breathing

"Belly Breathing"

Deep breathing through the nose, making the stomach rise, then slow exhale through the mouth.

spa

Pursed-Lip Breathing

"Calming Breath"

Inhale for 2 seconds, then exhale through puckered lips for 4 seconds. Great for COPD recovery.

Humidification

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I improve my lung capacity? expand_more
Regular aerobic exercise like swimming or brisk walking, combined with specific breathing exercises and staying hydrated, can significantly improve lung efficiency over time.
What are the early signs of lung issues? expand_more
Watch for persistent coughs, shortness of breath during light activities, recurring chest pain, or wheezing sounds during respiration.
